Fuel Cell Supply Chain Alliance

 

About us

Fuel Cell Supply-Chain Alliance is a partnership between government, industry and academia with a common vision to develop an eco-system of supply-chain for fuel cell manufacturing in Canada. Founded in 2014, the alliance is supported by two core partners:

  • Industry Canada
  • National Research Council

Canada is a global leader in the development of fuel cell technologies, due in large part to first-class expertise operating within strong and vibrant innovative clusters of companies and institutions. Canada also has well established automotive manufacturing sector with a comprehensive supply-chain eco-system. A preliminary assessment of the Canadian production and manufacturing capabilities conducted by the Automotive Parts Manufacturer Association (APMA) in consultation with Industry Canada indicated that there are significant capabilities and infrastructure within Canada to support fuel cell production and manufacturing. A concerted effort is required to coordinate and align the development of component specifications, codes and standards and translate those into business opportunities for the potential suppliers. Outreach activities are needed to promote the development of fuel cell supply-chain alliance in order to attract innovative manufacturing firms and top talents to participate.

  • The alliance aims to Develop a strong supply chain of low cost FC system components in Canada by bringing together fuel cell OEMs, current and potential suppliers through:
  • Identification of product specifications
  • Development of testing methods
  • Development of quality assurance and control methods

It is envisioned that these objectives can only be met through the collective efforts of various workgroups consisting of technical experts and business partners focusing on key topics that address the industrialization challenges of fuel cell technologies..

 

Participants

The fuel cell supply-chain alliance consists of voluntary participants who have capabilities in one or more of the following areas:

  • Rapid prototyping
  • Prototypes (Machining)
  • Analysis and Characterization
  • Technology Development Concept
  • Evaluation, expertise and independent third party
  • Testing Facilities
  • Machine supply
  • Tool Supply
  • Standards Office
